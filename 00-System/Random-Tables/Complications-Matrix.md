# Complications Matrix — Success with Complications

*AI Directive: When a player fails a non-combat check (Stealth, lockpicking, Persuasion/Deception/Intimidation, Sleight of Hand, traversal Athletics/Acrobatics, forgery, Investigation in a hostile space), do NOT default to "you fail" or "combat begins." Instead, rule **Success with Complications**: the PC achieves the stated goal, but you roll on the appropriate table below — Blighted Swamp for wilderness/fen attempts, Urban/Havenport for city attempts. The result escalates the fiction through cost, exposure, debt, terrain, or pursuit, NOT through immediate violence. Worked example: a rogue rolls badly to pick the lock on a Hyperion warehouse — the door opens, but you roll on Urban/Havenport and get "a [[Cronus Dominion]] patrol marks the party going in"; the heist proceeds, but heat is now on the bastion. Do NOT use this rule when the GM wants a clean critical-failure beat (a bone-snap, a triggered trap, a deliberate hard "no"), when the check was clearly succeeded, or when the fiction has already moved past the action being attempted.*

## Blighted Swamp Complications (1d8)
| d8 | Complication |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1** | A boot, a quiver, a spell focus, or a torch is sucked into the muck and lost. PC retrieves it on a successful DC 13 Strength (Athletics) check OR by spending 10 minutes and making noise — choose. |
| **2** | The path the PCs were on collapses behind them — a ten-foot stretch of bog gives way. The party reaches its goal, but the return route now requires a detour past [[Hex-1111\|Drownroot Hollow]] or a swim across a tidal channel. |
| **3** | Gear is soaked through. One held or worn non-magical item of the GM's choice (rations, spellbook, bowstring, paper map, a healer's kit) is ruined or unusable until a long rest spent drying it. |
| **4** | The frogs go silent. A telegraphed predator — a [[Hex-1111\|Drownroot Hollow]]-sized presence, *not the alligator itself* — has been drawn by the noise. The party still reaches their objective; on the way out, drag-furrows now cross their tracks. |
| **5** | Minor blight exposure. A PC who touched standing water or handled an item from the [[Hex-1110\|Gullcry Strand]] cairn contracts a mild Blight-taint: until their next long rest spent on consecrated or Concord-marked ground, they have disadvantage on Constitution saves against poison and disease. |
| **6** | A third party is on the same trail. Roll 1d3: **(1)** an [[Atlas Concord]] scout watching the [[Hex-1111\|Cypress Gate]] sigil sees the party and notes their faces; **(2)** [[Hyperion Syndicate]] smugglers caching at the [[Hex-1110\|Keeled Dory]] hear the noise and abandon the cache early, taking the ledger; **(3)** will-o'-wisps drift in and shadow the party for 1d4 hours, drawing other things' attention. None attack — yet. |
| **7** | Fog rolls off the fen. Visibility drops to 15 ft. for 1 hour. The party's destination is reached, but exit navigation requires a DC 13 Wisdom (Survival) check or the group emerges one hex off course (GM's choice — adjacent hex, half a day lost). |
| **8** | Sound carries across the still water. A nearby NPC or landmark now knows someone was here: [[Woody Haygrin]] at the fen's blind heard the splash and will mention it to the next Havenport patrol that asks; OR a fresh-burned [[Atlas Concord]] sigil appears on a tree along the party's exit route within the day, marking them as *seen*. |

## Urban / Havenport Complications (1d8)
| d8 | Complication |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1** | A city watch officer takes the bribe — and remembers the face. The PCs walk free, but they now owe a specific, unnamed favour to the [[Cronus Dominion]] patrol sergeant, callable at the worst possible moment. Track this as a debt on the bastion sheet. |
| **2** | A [[Hyperion Syndicate]] factor — one of [[Lady Vexis]]'s people — observes the job and recognises competence. Within 1d3 days a sealed offer of "discreet contract work" arrives at the party's bastion. Refusing it is its own answer. |
| **3** | A witness will testify later. A barmaid, a dockhand, a temple acolyte — pick one — saw enough to place the party at the scene. They will not act on it now, but the next time the [[Magistrate Theron\|Magistrate]] convenes the [[Havenport Concordat]] on a related matter, this person's name surfaces. |
| **4** | Gossip spreads. By dawn the story is in [[Marla Swiftpour]]'s tavern in distorted form. **Faction heat tick:** advance one faction's awareness clock by one (GM's choice — usually whichever faction the action most threatened). |
| **5** | The party is tailed home. A nondescript figure follows them back to their bastion, marks the door, and is gone before they can confront them. A faction now knows where they sleep. (Roll 1d3 for which: 1 [[Cronus Dominion]], 2 [[Hyperion Syndicate]], 3 [[Atlas Concord]] — vary across uses.) |
| **6** | A shop blacklists the party. The shopkeeper — [[Pip Underbough]] of [[The Steady Supply]], or [[Kaela Runeforge]] of [[The Ember Anvil]], whichever is closer to the action — quietly stops serving them at full prices. All purchases at that establishment cost +25% until restitution is made or the relationship is repaired. |
| **7** | An [[Atlas Concord]] researcher — likely [[Lyra Meadowlight]] — finds a thread the party left behind (a Blight-residue trace, a green-fletched arrow, a sigil rubbing). She does not report it to the Concordat yet, but she now has leverage and will spend it on a favour of her own choosing. |
| **8** | [[Commander Valerius]] himself is briefed. Not a manhunt — a *file*. The Iron Seat now has the party's names on a watch list. The next time they enter or leave the city gates carrying anything contraband (Blight-crystals, Syndicate cipher-ledgers, Concord relics), they are searched. |

## Using This Table
Pick the table by where the action happened — fen, coast, ruins, or wild lean Blighted Swamp; anything inside Havenport's walls or a satellite settlement leans Urban. If a rolled result doesn't fit the fiction, pick a neighbouring entry instead of re-rolling. The GM may also apply any result narratively without rolling when a single specific complication is obviously the right cost.
