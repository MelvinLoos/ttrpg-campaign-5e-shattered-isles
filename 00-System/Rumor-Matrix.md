# Rumor Matrix

*AI Directive: Rumors are the lifeblood of the West Marches sandbox. Each hex seeds three rumors: one **True**, one **Partial** (a true core wrapped in a misleading frame), and one **False**. Rumors are heard in taverns, on market day, from guards, from the drunk at the next table, from children. The GM decides who carries which.*

## Hex 1011 — Brineglass Wood (Forest, North of Havenport)
- **True:** "The Dominion's driving iron nails into the trees north of town. Straight line, chest-height, stamped with the sun. Somebody on the Verdant Seat is going to *taste blood* when they hear about it." — *Dockhand, off-shift, at the Last Lantern.*
- **Partial:** "Old Widow Brineglass up the north road took in sick and fled back to the mainland — left her whole shop standing, door open. Poor woman." — *Market gossip.* *(True core: the cottage is abandoned and intact. Misleading frame: there is no "Widow Brineglass," the owner's identity is unknown, and she did not leave by choice nor necessarily for "the mainland." The letter on her desk was addressed to Brother Caelum Ash.)*
- **False:** "Wolves have come back to the North Wood. Pack of twelve, moving together, killed a Dominion surveyor last week." — *A hunter, loudly, at the Last Lantern.* *(No wolf pack. No dead surveyor. A Dominion surveyor is under threat in the wood, but from a person, not a pack.)*

## Hex 1110 — Gullcry Strand (Coast, East of Havenport)
- **True:** "Someone's caching goods above the tide on Gullcry. Fresh stone on the sea-grass line. Gulls don't settle over it. If you want my guess, it's not salt-pork." — *A beachcomber, sober, selling mended rope at market.*
- **Partial:** "Edbert Thurlow's dory came apart on the Teeth nine days ago. Fog, bad tide, that's the east shore for you." — *Dockmaster's ledger, repeated by the dockmaster.* *(True core: Edbert's dory is wrecked on the east shore and he is missing. Misleading frame: the boat did not strike the Teeth — the keel was split cleanly, and Dominion-stamped nails were left inside the hull.)*
- **False:** "Pirates out of the eastern isles landed at Gullcry last moon and burned a fishing family out of their cottage. Concordat's keeping it quiet." — *A drunk, very earnestly, to anyone who will listen.* *(There are no pirates on Gullcry. There is no burned cottage. The real activity is a Syndicate cache, much quieter and much more deliberate.)*

## Hex 1111 — Drownroot Hollow (Swamp, Northeast of Havenport)
- **True:** "The fen off the northeast road has gone wrong. Acorns floating on water where no oak grows, and they're *warm* when you pick one up. Woody Haygrin'll tell you the same if you ask him plain." — *A Concord-leaning herbalist at market.*
- **Partial:** "There's a big gator in the Drownroot that's been taking deer. Concord druids put a ward on the cypress gate to keep it penned in — it's the safest it's been in years." — *A retired guard at the Last Lantern.* *(True core: there is a large alligator at Drownroot Hollow, and the Concord has burned a sigil into the cypress arch. Misleading frame: the sigil is not a containment ward on the gator — it's a boundary-marker keeping people out, and the Concord may in fact be feeding the gator to reinforce the boundary.)*
- **False:** "The fen's full of goblins on stilts, harvesting frogs for some witch further inland. Three of them jumped a trapper last week." — *A boatwright, repeating his cousin's story.* *(No goblins on stilts at Drownroot. The stilt-camp in the hex is a collapsed human fishing platform, weeks abandoned.)*

## Havenport Drift — Tavern & Market Rumors (Faction-Clock & Mission Chatter)

*AI Directive: These rumors are not bound to a single hex. They are loose Havenport chatter that ties directly to one of the active faction clocks in [[Active-Clocks]] or to one of the four open Tier-1 missions. Use them when a new party arrives at the Last Lantern, the Bounty Board crowd, or the market square and the table needs a tug toward the next tick. Truth tags follow the same convention as the per-hex rumors above (**True / Partial / False**).*

- **True:** "There's a Hyperion clerk asking around the Steady Supply after a man named Tully Marsh — old prospector, hand tremor, used to sell rough stone out the back door. Tully's gone three days now and his nets are still wet on the rack. The clerk paid in clipped silver and didn't blink when I asked twice." — *Pip Underbough, lowering his voice over the counter at the Steady Supply, both hands flat on the wood like he's measuring it.* *[GM: Pip is calm but deliberately slow — he wants the party to hear what he is not saying. Encountered when the party comes in to resupply or asks Pip about local prospectors. Drop voice, no eye contact at first, then look up on the last sentence.]* *(True: this is the Hyperion Syndicate's *Monopolize the Blight-Crystal Trade* clock advancing to **Step 2/6 — Bribe or silence local prospectors who get too close.** Tully saw something near a Blight-heavy ruin and Vexis's people are tying off the loose end. If the PCs ignore this, Tully will not be found, the Syndicate clock ticks unchecked toward refining and smuggling, and the next prospector who gets close goes the same way — quietly, without a body.)*
- **True:** "The Concord druids are walking the wood at night. Not patrolling — *measuring*. I saw two of them with a knotted cord between them, pacing south of the Old Haven Road, stopping at every third tree to press a palm flat to the bark. They didn't speak the whole time. They wouldn't see me; I made sure of that." — *Garrick Stonevein, at the Broken Trail, while cleaning a quarrel-head with a rag and not looking up.* *[GM: Garrick is matter-of-fact, almost bored — he's reporting, not gossiping. Encountered when the party comes to him for trail advice or monster lore. He delivers the line without ever putting down the rag; the only tell is that he stops cleaning on the word "measuring."]* *(True: this is the Atlas Concord's *Awaken the Primordial Guardian (Oakharrow)* clock advancing to **Step 2/6 — Map the extent of the root system.** Faelynn's scouts are surveying how far Oakharrow's roots run beneath Brineglass Wood and beyond. If the PCs ignore this, the Concord finishes the map alone, identifies a node big enough to channel uncorrupted energy into, and Step 3/6 begins without any non-Concord witness — meaning the awakening accelerates in secret and the eventual geological upheaval blindsides Havenport.)*
- **Partial:** "Valerius lost a whole survey crew in the north wood. Goblins came down out of the canopy and put three engineers in the dirt — that's why the Iron Seat's hiring. They don't say *bodies* on a public board, you understand, but a paid crew doesn't go quiet for nothing." — *A talkative carter at the Last Lantern, two cups in, holding up three fingers each time he says "three."* *[GM: Slurring slightly, leans across the table on the word "bodies," wants to be agreed with. Encountered at the Last Lantern in the evening crowd, especially if the party is reading the Bounty Board. He'll repeat the three-fingers gesture if anyone seems impressed.]* *(True core: a Cronus Dominion survey crew in Brineglass Wood has indeed been interfered with, and the Iron Seat is hiring — this is the **Hold the Blaze Line** mission. Misleading frame: nobody is dead. The interference is a single cut surveyor's ribbon and a redrawn chalk line, not a triple murder, and the interferer is a lone Atlas Concord scout, not a goblin ambush from the canopy. The carter is repeating a story that has grown two corpses in the telling.)*
- **Partial:** "Vexis's lot dumped a dead fisherman on Gullcry Strand — Edbert Thurlow, the dockmaster's cousin, beaten and left under a pile of rocks above the tide. They're using the cairn as a *grave marker*, half-tide, so the gulls clean him before anyone walks past. Whole east coast smells of it." — *A paranoid kelp-merchant at the smuggler's pier, slurring into his cup, glancing twice at the door before each sentence.* *[GM: Speaks fast, voice dropping at every other word, taps the rim of his cup with a fingernail when he says "grave marker." Encountered at the back end of the dock market or the cheaper end of the Last Lantern's bar. He'll deny he said any of it if a Syndicate clerk walks in.]* *(True core: Edbert Thurlow is missing-presumed-dead and the Hyperion Syndicate is responsible — Vexis's smugglers killed him because he saw the wrong cargo on the east coast. There is a stone cairn above the tide on Gullcry Strand — this is **The Cairn Above the Tide** mission. Misleading frame: the cairn is a Blight-crystal cache, not a grave; Edbert's body is in his wrecked dory on the Teeth, not under the rocks; and the cairn is fresh sea-grass concealment, not a half-tide burial. The merchant has fused two real, separate things — a missing fisherman and a strange new cairn — into one grislier story.)*
- **False:** "Magistrate Theron keeps a fourth seat in the Chamber of Echoes. Hidden behind the High Seat's chair, draped in black cloth — nobody sits in it, and that's the point. The Concordat votes three-to-one on everything that matters because the empty chair counts. Ask any guard who's swept the chamber after dark." — *Sister Mireille, a wandering lay-priestess of the Lantern, gripping the speaker's wrist with one cold hand at the market shrine.* *[GM: Wide-eyed, certain, not drunk — she believes every word. Encountered at the small Lantern shrine in the market square, usually accosting strangers between the fish stalls and the well. She gets quieter, not louder, when challenged.]* *(False: there are exactly four seats in the Chamber of Echoes — High, Iron, Gilded, Verdant — and they are all canonical, all occupied, all visible. There is no draped fifth chair, no secret veto. Sister Mireille has built a conspiracy out of a black mourning-cloth that was draped over Theron's chair for one council session months ago, after a death in his household. A juicy false lead the party may chase into the Town Hall — they will find nothing, but the Magistrate may notice they were looking, which is its own complication.)*
- **False:** "Something big is moving on the Teeth. Not the wreck, not Edbert's dory — *under* it. Two nights running, the watch on the seawall saw lights down deep, blue-green, drifting past the reef the wrong way against the tide. Old-timers say there's a sea-wyrm denning out there, and it's got a taste for the harbor now." — *Old Bregg, the one-eyed dockhand, holding court at the end of the Last Lantern's long bench.* *[GM: Theatrical, gestures with a clay pipe, milks the pause before "under it." Encountered at the Last Lantern in the late evening when he's already got an audience of two or three apprentices. He'll embellish further if anyone buys him a second cup.]* *(False: there is no sea-wyrm. There are no drifting lights on the Teeth. The reef is dangerous in the ordinary way and the only thing under the wrecked dory is rotting wood and four Dominion-stamped iron nails. A pure red herring — a party that charters a boat to investigate will find a cold night, no monster, and possibly a glimpse of the Hyperion smugglers working the cairn from the seaward side, which loops them back toward a real lead by accident.)*
