# Edbert Thurlow

**Occupation**: Fisherman (currently missing)

Edbert Thurlow is a Havenport fisher of middling years, stout of build, quiet of habit, and known along the harbor as a man who keeps his own counsel and mends his own nets. He worked a single-sail dory out of the east quay, running a short line along the near shore for flatfish and, lately, for salvage.

Nine days before the arrival of the current expedition, Edbert failed to return from a dawn run. His wife reported his absence to the Concordat's dockmaster; a routine inquiry was opened and no wreckage surfaced. The matter has since faded from public attention, though his name remains on the dockmaster's ledger as "at sea."

His boat — identifiable by the mended seam along its port gunwale and a small copper ring stamped with the dockmaster's mark — was in fact wrecked along Gullcry Strand (Hex 1110) and half-buried in the shingle. The clean split along its keel is inconsistent with the reef he is thought to have struck. Inside the hull, someone working quickly left behind four stamped iron nails of Dominion make.

Edbert's fate is, at time of writing, unknown.
