# Woody Haygrin

**Occupation**: Forager and Fen-trapper

> **"If the frogs go quiet, you run. If the water goes still in a wind, you climb. And don't, for any price, wade the Drownroot."**

Woody Haygrin is a halfling of weathered build and unbothered temper who keeps a small blind at the landward edge of the fen northeast of Havenport. He is most often seen in oiled canvas and a wide, sagging hat, a woven creel at his hip and a short fen-knife at his belt. He forages reeds, crabs, and medicinal roots, which he trades in Havenport on market days.

Woody speaks plainly and without flourish. He does not volunteer information to strangers, but he will not withhold a warning if he judges it needed. Travelers passing his blind on the way into the Drownroot often remark that his advice, though brief, tends to prove accurate within the hour.

He has lived at the fen's edge long enough to read it the way a sailor reads a sky. He knows which pools are safe to wade, which cypress will hold a rope, and which of the local beasts is hunting that week. What he does not openly discuss is the changes he has noticed lately — the early acorn fall in a fen that bears no oaks, the warm humming in the water, the green-fletched arrows he has found in deer.

Woody is not aligned to any faction, though he trades courteously with Atlas Concord scouts when they pass and pointedly does not see Dominion surveyors when they do.
