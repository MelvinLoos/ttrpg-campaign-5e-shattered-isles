# Atlas Concord

**Type**: Mayor

**Leader**: Faelen Wildheart

**Goals**: Balance & Harmony

**Category**: FACTION

The Atlas Concord believes that the key to surviving and thriving in the Shattered Isles is to live in harmony with its primal, untamed magic. Led by the wise elf Faelen Wildheart, this faction is not a force of conquest, but of stewardship. They seek to understand the delicate balance of the land, seeing the blight not as a plague to be eradicated, but as a wound to be healed. Their members, often druids, rangers, and scholars, dedicate themselves to research, preservation, and the subtle, spiritual work of attuning to the world's natural rhythms. To the Atlas Concord, the Isles are a sacred place, and their goal is to ensure that a new civilization does not simply dominate the land, but becomes a part of it.

# Brother Caelum Ash

**Occupation**: Librarian

**Category**: NPC

> **“What endures is not what was strongest, but what was remembered clearly.”**

Brother Caelum Ash is a familiar and reassuring presence within Havenport, known less for his visibility than for his constancy. He moves through the city with an unhurried calm, and those who seek him often do so not in moments of urgency, but at points where understanding feels just out of reach. His name is spoken with quiet respect, particularly among those who value context over immediacy.

Caelum is a tall, ash-haired man whose robes bear the faint traces of ink, soot, and candle wax. His appearance is modest, almost austere, yet well cared for. He carries himself with a posture shaped by long hours of reading and writing, and is rarely seen without a small bundle of notes or a book tucked beneath one arm.

In conversation, Caelum is patient and deliberate. He listens carefully, often allowing others to finish speaking before responding with a question of his own. His voice is steady and measured, and while he does not shy away from difficult truths, he presents them without judgment. Many find his manner calming, particularly when dealing with unfamiliar histories or conflicting accounts.

Brother Caelum serves as one of Havenport’s primary keepers of recorded knowledge. Travelers and residents alike turn to him when seeking to understand the history, cultures, or long-standing conflicts of the Shattered Isles. He does not claim absolute authority, and is quick to note when records are incomplete or contradictory. To Caelum, uncertainty is not a failure of knowledge, but an invitation to further inquiry.

He works closely with the city’s other figures, often directing those seeking practical preparation toward Garrick Stonevein or Kaela Runeforge, and referring travelers back to The Last Lantern when rest or reflection is needed before action. Though deeply knowledgeable, Caelum rarely presents himself as the final word on any subject.

Among the people of Havenport, Brother Caelum Ash is regarded as someone who helps others place themselves within a larger story — not to diminish their role, but to clarify it.

# Commander Valerius

**Occupation**: Military Commander & Faction Leader

**Appearance**: Polished plate armor, military cut, scarred but disciplined

**Personality**: Stern, authoritative, strategic, protective

**Background**: Career soldier dedicated to taming the chaos of the Isles

**Category**: NPC

> *"Chaos is not a force to be bargained with, soldier. It is a weed to be pulled. We do not explore these Isles to marvel at the ruins; we are here to lay the foundation for a civilization that will last a thousand years. Now, stand tall."*

Commander Valerius is the iron-willed leader of the **Cronus Dominion** forces in Havenport. He embodies the Dominion's philosophy that the Shattered Isles are a dangerous, untamed frontier that must be "domesticated" through discipline and structure.

He is a imposing human male in his late forties, with salt-and-pepper hair cut short in a strict military style. He is rarely seen out of his gleaming plate armor, which is polished to a mirror sheen and reinforced with the solid iron and stone aesthetics typical of his faction. A heavy cloak bearing the Dominion's sigil hangs from his shoulders, and he carries himself with the rigid posture of a career soldier who has spent a lifetime on the front lines.

His quarters in Havenport resemble a war room more than a home. Maps cover every wall, marked with strategic pins and supply lines, and the air smells of oil, steel, and old parchment. He is a man who believes that peace is the result of superior preparation and unyielding order.

### The General & The Tactician

Valerius acts as the primary contact for adventurers who believe in the rule of law and the necessity of hierarchy. He represents the Dominion's goal to bring stability to the region through "disciplined exploration".

As a quest giver, Valerius focuses on **Exploration and Control**. He tasks adventurers with clearing hexes of hostile monsters to establish safe trade routes, hunting down chaotic threats that endanger the settlement, and securing strategic locations for future forts. He has little patience for reckless glory-seeking; he rewards efficiency, teamwork, and results.

**Background**: Guardian of the Wilds seeking to heal the Sundered Isles

Faelynn Wildheart acts as the spiritual and operational heart of the **Atlas Concord**. She is rarely found behind a desk; instead, she is usually seen tending to the living architecture of Havenport or mediating disputes between settlers and the local fey.

Faelynn serves as the primary contact for adventurers seeking to understand the Shattered Isles rather than just exploit them. She represents the Atlas Concord’s goal to restore balance to the wild energies of the land.

# Garrick Stonevein

**Occupation**: Monster Hunter

**Category**: NPC

> **“If something out there is hunting people, it helps to know its habits before you know its name.”**

Garrick Stonevein is a figure most residents of Havenport recognize on sight, even if they have never spoken to him directly. He is known as a hunter in the oldest sense of the word — not a seeker of trophies, but a watcher of patterns. Those who come looking for him usually do so with purpose, and Garrick has little patience for idle curiosity when danger is involved.

He is a broad-built dwarf with a weathered face marked by old scars, the most noticeable being a pale line across his scalp and the absence of his left eyebrow. His gear is worn but meticulously maintained, and he is rarely seen without his heavy crossbow close at hand. There is nothing ornamental about his appearance; everything he carries has proven its worth.

In conversation, Garrick is direct and economical. He asks questions before offering answers, often more than people expect. While his manner can come across as gruff, those who spend time speaking with him soon realize that this reserve is born from caution rather than disinterest. He listens closely, especially when others speak of unfamiliar regions or strange encounters.

Within Havenport, Garrick serves as a point of reference for the dangers that lie beyond the city’s reach. Adventurers seek him out when rumors of monsters surface, when paths grow uncertain, or when something encountered in the wild does not behave as expected. He has little interest in glory and does not embellish his accounts; what he offers is assessment, not reassurance.

Garrick maintains working relationships with many of the city’s key figures. He is known to direct those seeking historical context toward The Common Record, and those in need of proper equipment toward The Ember Anvil before venturing out again. While he spends much of his time at The Broken Trail, he is often absent for days at a time, returning with new observations rather than stories.

Among Havenport’s residents, Garrick Stonevein is regarded as someone who has seen enough of the Shattered Isles to respect them — and expects others to do the same.

# Havenport

Havenport hums with **optimistic ambition and frontier tension**. Its appearance is one of **improvised efficiency and burgeoning permanence**, with docks bustling with vessels, and functional timber-and-stone buildings increasingly joined by more refined structures reflecting faction influence. The atmosphere is a melting pot of diverse pioneers, all working to reclaim the Isles, yet aware of the subtle rivalries of the Atlas Concord, Cronus Dominion, and Hyperion Syndicate that shape daily life and decisions under the **Havenport Concordat**.



------

# Havenport Concordat

**Function**: Executive Government of Havenport

**Headquarters**: The Chamber of Echoes (Town Hall)

**Motto**: Unity in Survival

> *"Three pillars support the roof of this city: Iron, Gold, and Wood. Remove one, and the structure leans. Remove two, and it collapses. It is my job to ensure they remain standing, even if they hate each other."* — **Magistrate Theron**

### Overview

The **Havenport Concordat** is the supreme governing body of the settlement of Havenport. Born out of necessity rather than friendship, it is a tripartite council presided over by an impartial Magistrate.

While Havenport is theoretically a neutral zone, in practice, it is a battleground of influence. The Concordat was established to prevent open warfare between the factions; it ensures that while they compete for the resources of the Shattered Isles, the city itself remains a functional sanctuary for all pioneers. Decisions made here affect taxes, gate laws, district expansions, and the deployment of the City Watch.

### Structure & Membership

The Council meets in the **Chamber of Echoes** within the Town Hall, a room designed with superior acoustics so that no whisper goes unheard. It consists of four seats:

1. **The High Seat (Executive):** Held by **Magistrate Theron**.
   * *Role:* He sets the agenda, mediates disputes, and holds the tie-breaking vote. He controls the "Neutral" assets, such as the Town Guard and the courts.
2. **The Iron Seat (Defense):** Held by **Commander Valerius** (Cronus Dominion).
   * *Role:* Oversees the city's defenses, the training of the militia, and the enforcement of martial law during crises. Valerius constantly pushes for stricter curfews and stronger walls.
3. **The Gilded Seat (Treasury):** Held by **Lady Vexis** (Hyperion Syndicate).
   * *Role:* Manages the city’s trade agreements, tax collection, and the market districts. Vexis argues that "growth" is the priority and frequently tries to buy votes or deregulate trade.
4. **The Verdant Seat (Sustenance):** Held by **Faelynn Wildheart** (Atlas Concord).
   * *Role:* Oversees food production, sanitation, and relations with the local wildlife/fey. Faelynn often vetoes industrial expansion if it threatens the natural balance of the region.

### The Political Dynamic

The Concordat is rarely in total agreement. It operates on a system of **"Tense Checks and Balances."**

* **Cronus vs. Hyperion:** Valerius wants to tax trade to fund defense; Vexis wants to cut defense spending to boost trade.
* **Cronus vs. Atlas:** Valerius wants to burn back the forest to clear fire lines; Faelynn forbids it.
* **Hyperion vs. Atlas:** Vexis wants to strip-mine nearby caves; Faelynn demands they be preserved as sacred sites.

It is up to **Magistrate Theron** to find the compromise that keeps the city running without alienating any single faction entirely.

# Hyperion Syndicate

**Type**: Mayor

**Goals**: Profit

**Category**: FACTION

The Hyperion Syndicate sees the Shattered Isles not as a home to be protected or a territory to be tamed, but as a market to be exploited. Led by the shrewd and opportunistic Tiefling, Lady Vexis, this faction is driven by a single-minded pursuit of profit and power. Their philosophy is that the chaotic energies and untapped resources of the Isles are an opportunity for immense wealth, and they will stop at nothing to acquire it. The Syndicate's members, comprised of mercenaries, merchants, and spies, operate with a cold pragmatism. They fund dangerous expeditions, manipulate rivals, and see every problem as a chance to turn a profit. To them, the land is just a commodity, and their success is measured not in glory or civic progress, but in the accumulation of gold and influence.

# Jaquess "Le Baron" Samedi

**Occupation**: Illicit Alchemist & Occult Banker

**Appearance**: Pristine Suit, Top Hat, Skull Mask

**Personality**: Theatrically Charming, Polite, Sinister

**Background**: Enigmatic Independent Wanderer

**Faction**: Independent

**Category**: NPC

> *"Do not worry about the coin, mon ami. Gold is heavy, burdensome. I offer you something lighter. A promise. A handshake. Do we have an accord?"*

In the rough and mud-stained frontier of the Shattered Isles, Jaquess Samedi is a jarring vision of civility. Known to most simply as "Le Baron," he is a wandering merchant and financier who seems entirely untouched by the grime of the wilds.

He appears as a man in his late twenties with rich, dark skin, invariably dressed in an immaculate, tailored black suit with velvet purple accents. He completes the ensemble with a silk top hat that never seems to fray. However, few can claim to have seen his true face, as he conceals the upper half behind a porcelain masquerade mask painted to resemble a stylized human skull.

Despite his charming smile and polite demeanor, there is an unsettling aura that clings to him. Birds tend to go silent when he approaches, and the air grows noticeably colder in his immediate vicinity. He is a figure of transaction and mystery, operating outside the laws of the major factions.

Le Baron is best known for two primary services. First, he is a master alchemist of the highest order. While he stocks standard healing droughts, his true inventory consists of "less than legal" magical wares: potent poisons, restricted spell components, and volatile experimental elixirs that the Bastions' guilds refuse to sanction.

Secondly, he acts as a lender to the desperate. For adventurers short on coin but rich in ambition, Jaquess offers significant loans of gold or equipment. He is known to be generous with his purse, though his methods of debt collection are rumored to be supernatural in nature.

# Kaela Runeforge

**Occupation**: Blacksmith

**Category**: NPC

> **“Steel remembers how it was treated. So do the people who carry it.”**

Kaela Runeforge is known throughout Havenport as a craftswoman of uncommon focus. While many recognize her by name, fewer would claim to know her well, and that distance appears intentional. Those who seek her out usually do so with a specific purpose, and Kaela respects clarity above all else.

She is a human woman with dark hair often tied back for work, her clothing marked by scorch patterns and carefully repaired wear. Faint runic symbols are burned into her heavy apron — not decorative, but practical — and her hands bear the marks of long years at the forge. When she moves, it is with economy, as if every action has already been considered.

Kaela speaks little while working, and even less without reason. When she does address others, her words are precise and measured, often focused on materials, limits, and intent. She listens closely to what is asked of her, but pays equal attention to what is left unsaid. Many find her reserved nature intimidating at first, though this impression often fades once her reliability becomes clear.

Within Havenport, Kaela is responsible for ensuring that those who venture beyond the city’s safety do so with equipment that will not fail them. She forges and repairs weapons and armor, and on occasion incorporates subtle enchantments when the need is justified. She does not believe every problem requires stronger steel, but she believes firmly that poorly made tools invite unnecessary risk.

Kaela maintains professional relationships with several of Havenport’s key figures. She frequently directs those seeking information about dangerous regions toward Garrick Stonevein, and those asking after the origins of unusual materials toward Brother Caelum Ash. While she has little interest in tavern talk, she is known to quietly listen when such conversations pass near her forge.

Among Havenport’s residents, Kaela Runeforge is regarded as someone who does not promise lightly — and whose work, once accepted, can be trusted completely.

# Lady Vexis

**Occupation**: Merchant Princess & Syndicate Leader

**Appearance**: Crimson Tiefling, gold-adorned horns, luxurious silks

**Personality**: Ambitious, calculating, opportunistic, transactional

**Background**: High-stakes investor seeking to monopolize the Isles' resources

**Category**: NPC

> *"Opportunity is a river, darling. You can either watch it flow by, or you can build a dam and charge people for the water. I suggest you start building."*

Lady Vexis is the calculating and glamorous face of the **Hyperion Syndicate** within Havenport. She treats the exploration of the Shattered Isles not as a crusade or a spiritual journey, but as the ultimate business venture.

She is a Tiefling of striking, infernal beauty, possessing deep crimson skin and eyes like molten gold. Her horns, which sweep back elegantly from her forehead, are often adorned with gold filigree or draped with fine jewelry chains. She favors high-collared gowns of silk and velvet that accentuate her lineage rather than hide it. Her long, spade-tipped tail moves with a mind of its own, often curling around her wine glass or tapping impatiently against the floor during negotiations.

Her quarters in Havenport are a stark contrast to the rough frontier, decorated in marble and gold to reflect the Syndicate's luxurious aesthetic. She carries herself with the undeniable confidence of someone who knows that in the Syndicate, gold holds more power than bloodlines.

### The Broker & The Diplomat

Lady Vexis acts as the primary contact for adventurers who view the Isles as a treasure chest waiting to be unlocked. She represents the Syndicate's ideology of ambition, profit, and the exploitation of raw magical resources.

As a quest giver, Vexis focuses on **Resource Extraction and Diplomacy**. She tasks adventurers with securing rare mining sites, negotiating trade agreements with neutral parties (or strong-arming them), and retrieving ancient artifacts not for a museum, but for the highest bidder. She is less interested in "saving the world" and more interested in who holds the deed to it.

# Magistrate Theron

**Occupation**: Magistrate & Head of Council

**Appearance**: Stern older human, official robes, chain of office

**Personality**: Pragmatic, impartial, overworked, no-nonsense

**Background**: The political glue holding the rival factions together

**Category**: NPC

> *"I do not care which banner you fly when you are out in the wilds. You can praise nature, profit, or order all you like. But within these walls? You answer to the law of Havenport. We survive together, or we perish separately. Now, sit down."*

Magistrate Theron is the pragmatic and steadfast anchor of Havenport. As the head of the **Havenport Concordat**—the tripartite council comprising the leaders of the three major factions—he holds the final executive authority over the settlement.

He is a human male in his late fifties, bearing the weight of the city on his shoulders. He has a stern, lined face framed by a neatly trimmed salt-and-pepper beard. He dresses in the official robes of the Magistracy—deep blues and greys—but they are often worn over practical travel clothes, suggesting a man ready to work, not just pontificate. A heavy chain of office made from the various metals found in the Isles hangs around his neck, symbolizing the unity of the settlement.

Theron is rarely found resting. He is usually seen in the Town Hall, surrounded by scribes, runners, and arguing faction representatives. He exudes an air of exhausted but unyielding authority, the "adult in the room" who keeps the ambitious faction leaders from tearing the city apart.

### The Administrator & The Arbiter

Theron acts as the primary quest giver for "General" or "Neutral" missions that benefit the settlement as a whole rather than a specific ideology. He is responsible for the town's day-to-day administration, law enforcement, and infrastructure.

He works to maintain a delicate balance between the **Atlas Concord**, **Cronus Dominion**, and **Hyperion Syndicate**. When a crisis arises that threatens the existence of Havenport—such as a massive siege or a magical plague—it is the Magistrate who issues the general call to action, overriding faction disputes to mobilize every able-bodied adventurer.

# Marla Swiftpour

**Occupation**: Tavernkeeper

**Category**: NPC

> *“Many roads find their way to Havenport. Most of them pass through the Lantern's door first.”*

Marla Swiftpour is one of those figures most travelers come to recognize long before they learn her name. She has a steady presence and an ease of movement that suggests long familiarity with Havenport’s rhythms, as if the city itself has taught her when to act and when to wait. Those who pass through often remember her not for anything she said, but for the feeling that nothing needed to be rushed in her presence.

She is a broad-shouldered woman, usually dressed for work rather than display, with dark hair kept simply tied back. A cloth is often draped over one shoulder, and her hands are rarely idle for long. There is little about her appearance that draws attention at first glance, yet few fail to notice how naturally others seem to orient themselves around her.

Marla speaks plainly and without flourish. She listens more than she talks, and when she does respond, it is with a clarity that suggests careful consideration rather than instinct. She does not offer counsel unless asked, and even then prefers to share what she knows rather than what she believes. Many find this restraint reassuring.

As the keeper of The Last Lantern, Marla has become a quiet constant in Havenport. Adventurers new and old alike pass through her care, often at moments of transition — arrival, return, or pause between journeys. Over the years, she has heard countless plans spoken aloud for the first time, and just as many reconsidered over a warm meal.

Though she maintains cordial relationships with all of Havenport’s notable figures, Marla rarely places herself at the center of events. Instead, she is known for guiding others toward the right doors, at the right time. Information passes through her tavern easily, but never carelessly.

It is sometimes said that while Havenport has many capable hands, Marla Swiftpour is one of the few who helps people remember why they came in the first place.

# Nessa Glowroot

**Occupation**: Arcanist

**Category**: NPC

> **“Magic grows best when it is given room, patience, and respect.”**

Nessa Glowroot is known in Havenport as a calm and steady presence, particularly among those who approach magic with caution rather than confidence. She is not the most visible of the city’s figures, nor the loudest voice in any discussion, yet her influence is quietly felt wherever magical practice touches daily life.

An orc of gentle bearing, Nessa moves with an unhurried grace that mirrors the natural rhythms she so often speaks of. Her features are soft, framed by hair that carries subtle hints of green and earth tones, and her clothing favors natural fibers adorned with simple sigils rather than arcane regalia. A faint scent of moss and herbs often accompanies her presence.

In conversation, Nessa is attentive and reassuring. She listens carefully, allowing others the space to articulate their intentions before responding. Within Havenport, Nessa serves as a guide for those seeking to understand or work with magic responsibly. Adventurers come to her for scrolls, minor magical items, or assistance in shaping enchantments to suit specific needs. She is particularly mindful of those new to magic, and takes care to ensure that curiosity does not outpace understanding.

She often consults Brother Caelum Ash when dealing with ancient or culturally sensitive magic, and refers those in need of practical preparation to Kaela Runeforge or Pip Underbough. Among the people of Havenport, Nessa Glowroot is regarded as someone who treats magic not as a tool to be mastered, but as a relationship to be maintained. It is often said that those who leave her guidance feel not more powerful, but more grounded.

# Pip Underbough

**Occupation**: Shopkeeper

**Category**: NPC

> **“Most journeys don’t fail for lack of courage. They fail because someone forgot the simple things.”**

Pip Underbough is widely regarded as one of Havenport’s most approachable figures, particularly among those new to the city or uncertain of their footing. He is known less for authority than for dependability, and many travelers find themselves speaking to him before they realize how much they have learned in the process.

A halfling of slight build, Pip is usually seen moving briskly between shelves and crates, an oversized pack often slung over his shoulders even when indoors. His clothing is practical and well-worn, mended carefully rather than replaced, and his expressions tend toward open curiosity. There is an ease to his presence that puts others at rest without demanding attention.

In conversation, Pip is friendly and attentive, with a habit of asking clarifying questions that feel more helpful than probing. He speaks in a straightforward manner, often using examples drawn from everyday experience. While he is quick to smile, he takes requests seriously, especially when someone seems unsure of what they need.

Within Havenport, Pip serves as a quiet constant for adventurers preparing to depart or regrouping after return. He is often the one who notices gaps in preparation — missing supplies, unrealistic expectations, or misunderstandings about what lies ahead. Rather than correcting others outright, he tends to offer options, allowing people to adjust their plans without embarrassment.

Pip maintains strong working relationships with the city’s other key figures. He regularly directs those in need of specialized gear to Kaela Runeforge, and those asking after routes or dangers to Garrick Stonevein. When questions turn toward the unfamiliar or historical, he readily refers visitors to the Common Record.

Among Havenport’s residents, Pip Underbough is appreciated as someone who makes preparedness feel manageable. It is often said that while others help adventurers face the extraordinary, Pip ensures they are not undone by the ordinary.

# The Broken Trail

**Type**: Monster Hunter Lodge

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“Every path looks survivable, until you stop to study where the others ended.”**

The Broken Trail stands slightly apart from Havenport’s busiest streets, positioned where the city begins to thin into paths that lead outward rather than inward. Its name is carved plainly into a weathered sign, with no embellishment or motto beneath. Those who arrive here usually know why they have come.

The interior of the Broken Trail is practical and subdued. Maps line the walls, many marked with notes, scratches, or careful annotations. Shelves hold hunting gear, spare tools, and records of past expeditions, organized for use rather than display. The space carries the faint scent of oiled metal and old leather, and conversations tend to be quiet and focused.

The Broken Trail exists to prepare people for what lies beyond Havenport’s borders. It is a place where dangers are discussed honestly and without bravado, and where rumors are weighed against experience. Those who enter seeking guidance are often asked to describe what they have seen before being told what others know.

Visitors to the Broken Trail can expect to find information about hostile creatures, hazardous regions, and patterns of movement observed across the Shattered Isles. Discussions here are rarely dramatic, but often decisive. Many expeditions have been altered, delayed, or abandoned altogether after time spent within these walls.

Garrick Stonevein is most closely associated with the Broken Trail, and his influence is evident throughout the space. His approach favors preparation over reaction, and the atmosphere reflects that philosophy. On days when Garrick is absent, others still use the lodge, but the pace slows, as if waiting for his return.

Among those who travel far from Havenport, the Broken Trail is spoken of with quiet respect. It is said that many paths into the wild seem clear at first — and that this is the place where they are most often broken down, understood, and survived.

# The Common Record

**Type**: Library

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“What is written here is not truth itself, but the closest thing left behind.”**

The Common Record occupies a quiet stretch of Havenport, set back from the louder thoroughfares and marked by a simple, well-kept façade. There is little to draw attention to it from afar, and that appears to be by design. Those who enter do so intentionally, often seeking answers that cannot be rushed.

Inside, the Record is orderly and subdued. Shelves of books, scrolls, and ledgers line the walls, arranged with careful consistency rather than grandeur. Light filters in through high windows, softened by dust and age, and the dominant sounds are the turning of pages and the occasional murmur of quiet discussion. The space invites focus without demanding silence.

The Common Record exists to preserve and share what is known of the Shattered Isles — their histories, cultures, migrations, conflicts, and recoveries. It is a place where knowledge is treated as a shared responsibility rather than a guarded resource. Records here are maintained, cross-referenced, and amended as new information comes to light.

Visitors to the Common Record can expect to find guidance on regional histories, cultural practices, and the broader context behind present circumstances. Questions are encouraged, though answers are often layered rather than singular. It is not uncommon for a visit to the Record to end with a clearer understanding of what is *not* known, alongside what is.

Brother Caelum Ash’s influence is evident throughout the space. His emphasis on clarity and honesty shapes both the organization of the records and the manner in which information is shared. When he is present, conversations tend to slow, allowing ideas to settle before conclusions are drawn.

Among scholars and travelers alike, the Common Record is known as a place where memory is treated with care. It is said that while the Shattered Isles are fractured by distance and danger, their stories remain connected here — written not to glorify the past, but to ensure it is not forgotten.

# The Ember Anvil

**Type**: Armory

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“Nothing forged here promises victory, only that it will not fail you.”**

The Ember Anvil stands close enough to Havenport’s center to hear the city breathe, yet far enough from its quieter spaces that the sound of hammer on steel does not intrude. From the outside, the forge appears unadorned, its stone walls darkened by years of heat and smoke. The glow from within is steady rather than dramatic.

Inside, the Ember Anvil is warm, bright, and unmistakably active. The forge itself dominates the space, its embers carefully maintained. Tools hang in deliberate order along the walls, and unfinished pieces rest where they can be seen and assessed rather than hidden away. The air smells of heated metal, oil, and ash, and the rhythm of work gives the space its own tempo.

The Ember Anvil exists to ensure that Havenport’s adventurers are properly equipped for what lies beyond its walls. It is a place of preparation rather than invention, where equipment is adapted to purpose and limitations are discussed openly. Those who enter without a clear request are often encouraged to return once they know what they need.

Visitors to the Ember Anvil can expect practical assessments of their gear, honest advice about its condition, and access to weapons and armor built to endure sustained use. While the forge is capable of exceptional craftsmanship, it is not a place of spectacle. Items produced here are valued for reliability rather than ornamentation.

Kaela Runeforge’s presence defines the Ember Anvil completely. Her standards shape both the work produced and the expectations placed upon those who commission it. When she is absent, the forge remains operational, but its pace slows, as if unwilling to rush without her oversight.

Among those who have traveled the Shattered Isles, the Ember Anvil is known as a place where equipment is treated with the same seriousness as the lives that depend on it. It is often said that weapons forged here do not seek glory — they simply do not fail.

# The Last Lantern

**Type**: Tavern

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“No one asks how far you’ve come, only whether you wish to sit a while.”**

The Last Lantern stands along one of Havenport’s most traveled streets, its namesake light kept burning regardless of hour or weather. For many, it is the first sign that a journey has truly ended — or at least paused. The glow from its windows is visible from a distance, steady and unchanging.

Inside, the tavern is arranged with quiet intention. The space is open enough to welcome groups, yet never so crowded that a lone traveler feels out of place. Wooden beams and well-worn tables bear the marks of long use, and the air carries a mixture of cooked meals, clean wood, and the faint trace of sea wind carried in by the door. Conversation flows easily here, rising and falling without ever fully overtaking the room.

The Last Lantern serves as Havenport’s primary place of rest and informal gathering. Newcomers often find themselves there by chance, while returning adventurers arrive by habit. Plans are discussed, routes compared, and stories exchanged — not for acclaim, but for understanding. It is a place where one can listen without committing, and speak without performing.

Those who spend time in the Lantern quickly learn that questions asked here rarely go unanswered, though they are often redirected. The tavern is not a place of expertise, but of orientation. A conversation begun at one table frequently ends with a name, a place, or a path worth following elsewhere in the city.

The presence of Marla Swiftpour defines the Lantern as much as its walls do. Her steady oversight keeps the space calm without making it rigid, and regulars remark that the tavern feels subtly adrift on the rare occasions she is absent.

Among travelers, the Last Lantern is spoken of as a place where one can arrive without explanation and leave without obligation. It is said that no matter how far one wanders into the Shattered Isles, there is always a light waiting here, kept not as a signal — but as a promise.

# The Shattered Isle

**Type**: World

**Category**: LOCATION

### **The Heroes' Guild Presents: The Shattered Isles**

Welcome, pioneer, to the **Heroes' Guild** and the untamed lands of the **Shattered Isles**. Your journey begins in a world that was not born, but broken.

Long ago, a cataclysm known as **the Sundering** tore the old world asunder, reshaping the continent into a vast, spiraling archipelago of raw, untamed magic. In its wake, a powerful **Divine Covenant** was forged: true death is forbidden within the Isles. The fallen are recalled and reborn, their souls returning to the nearest **Bastion**, a fortified settlement that serves as both a safe haven and a frontier outpost.

You are one of the brave adventurers who have answered the call to explore this new world. As a member of the Heroes' Guild, you are not a lone hero, but a vital part of a grand expedition. The campaign is a **West Marches-style adventure**, which means your mission is not to follow a single linear story, but to venture out from the safety of Havenport, explore the unknown, and write the history of the Isles as you go.

The world is living, breathing, and dangerous. Three powerful factions vie for control, but not with open war. Their conflict is a tense dance of politics, resources, and influence, with the fate of the Isles hanging in the balance:

* **The Atlas Concord** seeks harmony with the land's primal magic.
* **The Cronus Dominion** strives to bring order to the chaos.
* **The Hyperion Syndicate** sees a world ripe for profitable exploitation.

Your character will be a direct agent in this struggle. Your actions will determine which faction gains power, which secrets are uncovered, and whether the Isles can be truly settled, or if their wild magic will consume all who dare to enter.

The world beyond Havenport's walls is waiting to be discovered. Which path will you forge?

# The Steady Supply

**Type**: General Store

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“Most journeys begin with grand plans, and are saved by simple things.”**

The Steady Supply occupies a well-traveled corner of Havenport, positioned where foot traffic naturally slows. Its exterior is unassuming, marked by a simple sign and wide windows that allow passersby to see shelves stocked in orderly abundance. The door is rarely closed for long.

Inside, the shop is bright and thoughtfully arranged. Crates and shelves are labeled clearly, and commonly needed items are kept within easy reach. The space feels active without being overwhelming, and there is a noticeable absence of clutter despite the wide variety of goods on offer. The scent of rope, dried herbs, oil, and clean fabric lingers in the air.

The Steady Supply exists to ensure that travelers and residents alike have access to the essentials required for daily life and long journeys. It is a place of provision rather than specialization, and its strength lies in reliability. Those who enter with uncertainty often leave with a clearer sense of what they need — and what they do not.

Visitors can expect to find general adventuring supplies, travel necessities, and everyday goods suited to a wide range of needs. Questions are welcomed, and advice is offered without judgment. The shop is often a first stop for newcomers, and a habitual one for those who have learned the value of preparation.

The presentation and displays of Pip Underbough really define the Steady Supply’s atmosphere. His emphasis on clarity and accessibility shapes both the layout of the shop and the manner in which services are offered. Regular patrons note that even during busy hours, the space retains a sense of calm order.

Among travelers passing through Havenport, the Steady Supply is known as the place where plans become practical. It is said that no one leaves the city fully prepared — but those who start here tend to fare better than most.

# The Verdant Sigil

**Type**: Arcanery

**Category**: LOCATION

> **“Magic does not ask to be mastered, only understood well enough to be trusted.”**

The Verdant Sigil is situated where Havenport’s stonework begins to give way to patches of green and open air. From the outside, the structure appears almost grown rather than built, its walls softened by climbing plants and natural curves. The symbol above its entrance — a simple, leaf-like sigil — is unassuming but unmistakable.

Inside, the space feels markedly different from the rest of the city. Light filters through high windows and living growth, casting shifting patterns across the floor. Shelves hold scrolls, crystals, and small enchanted objects, interspersed with potted plants and natural arrangements. The air is cool and carries the scent of earth, bark, and faintly charged energy.

The Verdant Sigil exists to provide access to magic that is intentional, stable, and considerate of its impact. It is not a place of experimentation or spectacle, but one of alignment and care. Those who enter are encouraged, subtly but clearly, to consider not only what they wish to accomplish, but why.

Visitors can expect to find magical items, scrolls, and guidance suited to a wide range of experience levels. Enchantments offered here favor utility, protection, and harmony over destructive force. Conversations often focus on balance, limits, and long-term consequences, though never in a discouraging manner.

The influence of Nessa Glowroot shapes every aspect of the Verdant Sigil. Her approach ensures that the space remains calm and grounded, even when powerful magic is present. Regular visitors note that time seems to move differently here, slowing just enough to allow thought to catch up with intent.

Among those who practice magic across the Shattered Isles, the Verdant Sigil is known as a place where power is treated with humility. It is said that magic taken from this place carries not only enchantment, but care — and that this makes all the difference.
