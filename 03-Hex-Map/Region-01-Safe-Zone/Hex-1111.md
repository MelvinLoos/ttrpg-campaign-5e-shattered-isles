# Hex 1111: Drownroot Hollow

## Environment & Terrain
**Biome:** Swamps & Wetlands (The Blighted Fens)

Northeast of Havenport, the forest's root system loses to the sea. The land flattens, the pines give way to moss-beard cypress and knotted mangrove, and the black-shingle coast disintegrates into a brackish tidal fen. Water here is never quite still — it breathes with the tide, rising along the cypress knees at dusk and retreating at dawn, leaving a slick line of salt-rime on every trunk.

The air is warmer than it should be for this time of year, and thick enough to chew. Frogs are loud past any reasonable measure. Mosquitos are not; the usual clouds are absent, which is its own wrongness. Where the fen meets solid ground, the earth is chopped and churned — long, slow furrows raked through the mud.

**A local forager, [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/NPCs/Woody-Haygrin|Woody Haygrin]], keeps a blind at the fen's edge and will warn any party he sees approaching: *"If the frogs go quiet, you run. If the water goes still in a wind, you climb. And don't, for any price, wade the Drownroot."*** He will point out the drag-furrows himself.

## Landmarks & Discoveries
- **The Cypress Gate (Icon):** A natural arch of two cypresses grown together over the fen-path, their bark silvered by salt. On the inland face of the left trunk, burned deep into the wood, is a **druidic sigil of Keeping** — fresh, the char still soft. A Concord mark.
- **Drownroot Hollow (Landmark):** [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/Locations/Drownroot-Hollow|Drownroot Hollow]] — the deep-water pool at the hex's heart, ringed with cypress knees and floating acorns. This is the hunting ground of the alligator.
- **The Acorn Fall (Hidden):** Hundreds of green, unripe acorns carpet the fen floor under trees that bear no oaks. They float on the standing water in slow, deliberate drifts, seeming to move against the current. Touching one: warm. Held for a moment: faintly humming.
- **The Drag-Furrows (Icon):** Three long, parallel gouges through a mudbank, each ending in water. At the deepest furrow, a half-chewed red deer, one leg and rib-cage, bubbling with flies. Fresh — hours old.
- **The Fisher's Stilt-Camp (Hidden):** A collapsed fishing platform, weeks abandoned, rope still knotted to the piling. One boot, still laced, in the shallow.

## The Living World (AI Instruction: MUST COMPLETE)
- **Clock/Secret Tie-in — Atlas Concord: "Awaken the Primordial Guardian (Oakharrow)" (Step 1/6):** The unseasonal acorn-fall in a treeless fen, the humming warmth of the seeds, and the freshly-burned druidic sigil of Keeping on the Cypress Gate are the first faint signs that Concord druids are mapping a node of Oakharrow's dormant root system — and that the root system is *responding*. Concord scouts have been through here recently, marking the boundary of what must not be disturbed. If the party stays quiet and still near Drownroot Hollow at dusk, they may hear, underneath the frogs, a slow vegetal groan like timber shifting under great weight. It is not the alligator.

## Encounters (Danger Level: Tier 1 — TELEGRAPHED DEADLY)
*Rolled 2d12 on Swamps & Wetlands table: **2 + 2 = 4** — **A giant alligator death-rolling a deer in the shallows.***

> **⚠ GM NOTE — DEADLY RESULT, HEAVILY TELEGRAPHED.** A giant alligator scaled to Tier 1 is still a party-wipe if a careful group walks into its water. This encounter must be **avoidable** through observation.

- **Creatures/Obstacle:** **1 Giant Alligator** (statted as *Crocodile*, CR 1/2, Tier 1 — use Crocodile stat block; if the party directly antagonizes it, it is the apex of this fen). It is currently occupied with a deer carcass at Drownroot Hollow and will not actively hunt unless a PC enters the water of the Hollow or bleeds near it.
- **Telegraphs layered from the hex edge inward:**
  1. **Woody Haygrin's warning** at the fen's edge ("don't wade the Drownroot").
  2. **Silence of frogs** within 60 ft of the Hollow — any Perception check notices.
  3. **The drag-furrows** and half-chewed deer, hours fresh.
  4. **The abandoned stilt-camp and single boot** — someone else trusted this water and did not come back.
  5. **A low, wet cough-growl** from the Hollow at any movement near its bank.
- **Treasure/Loot:** On the dead fisher's stilt-camp piling, a copper ring stamped with a Havenport dockmaster's mark. In the deer-carcass, a broken arrow — *fletched green*, an Atlas Concord marker, suggesting a Concord scout drove the deer toward the water as a deliberate feeding. Within Drownroot Hollow itself (requires drawing the alligator off, or patient low-tide retrieval): a single, intact, still-warm acorn the size of a fist.

## Notes for the GM
- If the party ignores every telegraph and wades in, that is their choice — but it must remain a choice made against *visible* evidence.
- The green-fletched arrow is the bridge between this hex's clock clue and its encounter. A sharp party will realize the Concord is *feeding* the gator to keep people out of the Hollow.
- Woody Haygrin knows more than he says but will not volunteer it to strangers.
