# Hex 0102: Brineglass Wood

## Environment & Terrain
**Biome:** Forest & Woodlands (The Verdant Coast)

The land north of Havenport climbs in a slow, even grade into a belt of salt-tolerant pine and black oak. Sea wind still combs this far inland, and the needle carpet hisses under it even on still days. Light falls in long, green columns through the canopy, and the air is heavy with the smell of sap, cold stone, and — where the wind shifts — the distant brine of the coast.

The old trail out of Havenport's North Gate is the only maintained path, and it is maintained poorly. Roots have broken the flagstones in a dozen places, and moss grows in the wheel ruts. The deeper one travels, the more the forest begins to feel watched — not maliciously, but attentively, in the way a working forest watches an intruder who does not yet know the rules.

## Landmarks & Discoveries
- **The Old Haven Road (Landmark):** The cracked, flag-paved trail that runs north out of Havenport and is slowly being reclaimed by the wood.
- **Brineglass Cottage (Landmark):** [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/Locations/Brineglass-Cottage|Brineglass Cottage]] — a slate-roofed alchemist's cottage set a short way off the trail, its chimney cold and its door unbarred. Currently abandoned; the garden is overgrown but the distillery apparatus remains.
- **The Dominion Blazes (Icon):** A line of freshly-stamped iron nails driven at chest-height into trunks along a surveyed path, each struck with the same rayed-sun maker's mark. A red surveyor's ribbon has been tied to one, then cut through with a knife.
- **The Listening Stump (Hidden):** An enormous oak stump, its rings still sharp with sap. Axe marks are recent, the trunk nowhere in sight. Standing on it, one can hear the sea faintly — louder than simple distance should allow.

## The Living World (AI Instruction: MUST COMPLETE)
- **Clock/Secret Tie-in — Cronus Dominion: "Iron Line" (Step 2/6):** The stamped iron nails, the cut surveyor's ribbon, and the missing oak are the leading edge of the Dominion's planned paved supply road. A careful traveler will notice that the blaze line runs *straight* — ignoring springs, burrows, and a badger's set — exactly as an engineer's line would. Whoever cut the ribbon was not the crew that placed it. Someone in the wood is already pushing back, quietly.

## Encounters (Danger Level: Tier 1)
*Rolled 2d12 on Forest & Woodlands table: **5 + 6 = 11** — An Alchemist's Cottage (50% chance abandoned, 50% occupied by a hermit). Coin flip: **Abandoned.***

- **Creatures/Obstacle:** No hostile creature encounter. The primary feature is **Brineglass Cottage**, abandoned but not ransacked. Evidence suggests the owner left in an orderly hurry — dried herbs still hang, a kettle sits empty on a cold hearth, a ledger is missing its last several pages.
- **Treasure/Loot:** A functional but simple distillery, 2 doses of *antitoxin* in stoppered vials on a shelf, a half-finished letter addressed to "B.C.A." at the Common Record, and a locked strongbox under a loose floorboard (DC 13 Thieves' Tools; contains 14 gp, a silver stirring rod, and a pressed leaf of an herb no one in Havenport recognizes).

## Notes for the GM
- The Dominion blaze-line is a **clue, not a quest board.** Let the players notice the stamped nails and the severed ribbon; do not explain them.
- If the party lingers past dusk, distant axe-fall can be heard from the northeast — two strokes, a pause, two strokes. Dominion engineers working late.
- The letter in the cottage may later tie the missing alchemist to [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/NPCs/Brother-Caelum-Ash|Brother Caelum Ash]], at GM discretion.
