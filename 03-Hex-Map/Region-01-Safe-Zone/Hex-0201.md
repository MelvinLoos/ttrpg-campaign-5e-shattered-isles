# Hex 0201: Gullcry Strand

## Environment & Terrain
**Biome:** Coastlines & Seas (The Shattered Shores)

East of Havenport the coast turns hard. The soft harbor beaches give way to a long, curving strand of black shingle, broken pillars of sea-cut basalt, and tide pools that drain with a loud suck between swells. Gulls are thick here — their cry is constant, rising in great white sheets whenever anything moves along the rocks — and the wind off the open water smells of iodine and old kelp.

The tideline is a salvager's strip. Rope, staved barrels, broken spar-wood and the occasional cracked figurehead come ashore here with the regularity of a market day. At low tide a flat of wet black sand opens out to a reef that locals call the Teeth, visible as a dark line under the breakers. Ships that get too close in fog do not usually come back.

## Landmarks & Discoveries
- **Gullcry Strand (Landmark):** The shingle beach itself, loud with birds. Visible from the Havenport road east.
- **The Teeth (Icon):** A line of jagged reef exposed at low tide. Any Havenport fisher can point it out and will tell you, unprompted, not to swim east of it.
- **The Keeled Dory (Hidden):** A small fishing boat half-buried in wet sand, hull split along the keel. The break is clean — too clean for a reef. Inside the hull, caught in a seam, are four black iron nails stamped with a rayed-sun maker's mark. The boat belonged to [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/NPCs/Edbert-Thurlow|Edbert Thurlow]], a Havenport fisherman who has been missing nine days.
- **The Cairn Above The Tide (Secret):** A low, fresh-built stone cairn set just above the high-tide line, partially hidden in sea-grass. Digging reveals a crate of straw-packed, faintly-glowing **Blight-crystals**, sealed in wax-cloth. A second crate beneath it. Neither is marked.

## The Living World (AI Instruction: MUST COMPLETE)
- **No active-clock clue in this hex.** (The Iron-Line and Oakharrow clues are seeded in Hex 0102 and Hex 0202.) What this hex carries instead is a live **Hyperion Syndicate** operation — a Blight-crystal cache being quietly planted by smugglers. A sharp-eyed party will notice the Keeled Dory's nails match the stamp seen on Dominion surveyor-marks inland; someone is selling Dominion ironmongery to parties who should not have it.

## Encounters (Danger Level: Tier 1)
*Rolled 2d12 on Coastlines & Seas table: **4 + 4 = 8** — Syndicate smugglers (1D4) frantically burying crates of Blight-crystals.*

- **Creatures/Obstacle:** **3 Syndicate smugglers** (Bandits, CR 1/8 — Tier 1). Two are digging at the cairn site above the tide; one is posted on a basalt pillar as a lookout. They are jumpy, not committed — if spotted, they will first try to bluff ("oyster diggers, move along"), then flee along the strand eastward if threatened. They will fight only if cornered or if the crates are touched.
- **Treasure/Loot:** 2 crates of raw, unrefined **Blight-crystals** (hazardous, unstable — any violent impact calls for a DC 12 Dex save or 1d4 necrotic damage per person in 5 ft.), 1 waxed ledger listing drop coordinates in a substitution cipher, 8 gp in mixed coin on the smugglers, and — if [[../../01-Campaign-Bible/Entities/Locations/Gullcry-Strand|Gullcry Strand]]'s tide pools are searched — 1d4 salvageable pieces of flotsam.

## Notes for the GM
- Let the gulls do the telegraphing. When the party first comes into line-of-sight of the cairn, a sheet of gulls lifts *away* from the workers — an unnatural movement that a Perception check will flag.
- The Keeled Dory and the stamped nails are a **slow-burn clue**, not a billboard. The connection to Hex 0102's blaze-line should be the players' discovery.
- If the smugglers escape, note it — Lady Vexis's people now know an adventuring party is working this coast.
